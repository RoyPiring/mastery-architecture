> *A hands-on route from amateur to command in residential architecture, one build at a time. A route to command, not a degree.*

I'm learning residential architecture the way it actually sticks: building the field end to end, one system at a time, with AI as the multiplier that makes real depth possible on my own hours. Not chapters I skim and forget, but working command I can show, and it grows in the open with every build.

The builds that best show the field will sit here as they land.

## What it covers

Computational and generative design, building-performance simulation, BIM, and prefab and digital fabrication.

**What it isn't.** Not a degree, and no claim past what each build proves. These builds are study and portfolio mock-ups, not stamped construction documents, engineering certifications, or code approvals. Life safety decisions require the licensed professionals responsible for them, and the title architect is legally protected.
